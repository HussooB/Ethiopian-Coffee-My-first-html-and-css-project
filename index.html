Our story

Kaldi noticed that his goats became energetic after eating berries from a tree, and he tried the berries himself, feeling alert and excited. He then took the berries to a nearby monastery, where the monks initially threw them into a fire. However, they realized their mistake and instead placed the berries in hot water to preserve them. The monks drank the resulting beverage and found that it tasted delicious and helped them stay awake during long nights of prayer. The knowledge of the energizing berries spread, and coffee cultivation began in Ethiopia in the 1500s. Today, Ethiopia is the world's sixth-largest coffee producer.
